# Expanded engineering / protocol layer

## Author-flow dependency analysis

This case exposes three major author assumptions:

1. The book is read in sequence.
2. The reader can flip back freely.
3. The reader still carries earlier discussion in mind.

These assumptions are safe in a continuous-reading book but unsafe after fine excerpting.

## Continuous-book assumptions vs excerpt-library assumptions

### Continuous-book assumptions
The author can write:
`تقدم الكلام عن الخمر...`
and trust the reader to recall or revisit the earlier passage.

### Excerpt-library assumptions
The owner may open one excerpt cold, with:
- no fresh memory of the book
- no chapter opened nearby
- no certainty about what exact earlier text is being referenced

That changes the sufficiency standard completely.

## Orientational summary vs concept-building summary

### Orientational summary
A note that mainly says: “we already passed this topic.”
Minimal support may be enough.

### Concept-building summary
A note that says: “remember the earlier concept, because I am now extending it.”
This requires stronger recovery support.

The current case is the second kind. The note is not merely telling the owner where they are. It is actively leaning on prior definition and disagreement material and a previously mentioned أثر.

## Why this specific case is not just a light reminder

The phrase:
`وأن الصحيح: أن الخمر كل ما خامر العقل...`
shows that the current passage is continuing and tightening a prior conceptual discussion. That makes the earlier discussion structurally relevant, not optional background.

## Context-restoration doctrine

What is needed here is not just a cleaner note. What is needed is a mechanism that restores the path the author assumed.

Candidate mechanisms include:
- openable exact earlier passage
- full passage visibility
- chapter reference layer
- linked excerpt cluster
- context note as secondary aid

## Passage-linkage doctrine

The key missing link is between:
- the excerpt currently open
- the exact earlier passage the author is invoking

This linkage must be viewable from inside the unit.

## Teaching-unit expansion pressure

This case pushes the system beyond “pure excerpt as quote.”

The source text remains central, but the unit may also need:
- guidance
- linkage
- recoverable original passage
- context support

This is the pressure toward “teaching unit.”

## What should remain centered

- the original source text
- the exact passage being relied on
- the author’s own flow

## What should be added around it

- explicit passage linkage
- reference restoration
- bounded contextual help

## What would be reckless to flatten

- assuming note sufficiency transfers unchanged from book to excerpt-library
- treating all reference-back notes as equivalent
- treating this as only a UI note problem
- forgetting that the owner expects the system to provide the path back

## What would be reckless to automate blindly

- auto-generating short summary notes and declaring the case solved
- collapsing concept-building references into light orientation cues
- removing passage-level access because excerpt links exist
- expanding teaching units without explicit discipline
