Detailed analysis:
My reaction is that this does not work as a standalone entry. The excerpt as given is doing more than one job at once, and one of those jobs is incomplete. The first part — `واختلف العلماء في حقيقة اليد التي تقطع على أقوال:` — is an unbiased marker that this issue is one of disagreement. That answers one question: is this a subject of خلاف at all? That can stand on its own as a very important kind of excerpt, because knowing that an issue is genuinely disputed is itself part of fiqh understanding.

But then the excerpt immediately moves into `وأصحها، ما ذهب إليه الجمهور،`. That is not the same job. That is no longer just marking that there is disagreement. That is a biased tarjih, and tarjih is attribution-critical because it tells me what this particular scholar is preferring. So I do not want those two functions collapsed into one entry.

The deeper problem is that the tarjih part is also clipped as given. It tells me that the strongest view is what the majority held, but it does not finish the thought here. So as a standalone entry I am left with a fused disagreement-marker plus an incomplete tarjih lead-in. That is not a clean excerpt for me.

So my judgment is not just that it feels short. It is that the excerpt as given is functionally mispackaged. The disagreement mention should be separable. If there were a full unbiased listing of the opinions, that would still naturally live with the disagreement layer because those are too tightly linked. But the scholar’s tarjih is a different question and should not stay fused to that marker. The surrounding context helps diagnose and repair this in analysis, but it does not rescue the excerpt as currently given.
