# Purpose and scope

This dossier analyzes **G1 — a very short structural transition**. It is collection-side material only, not promoted doctrine.

# Layer separation

## Owner-faithful questionnaire layer
The owner answer must preserve the tension that the excerpt feels weak and even "very useless" as a standalone piece, while still concluding that shortness alone does not justify rejection and that the line can carry value.

## Engineering / protocol layer
The deeper issue is not length preference. It is how to evaluate **added benefit**, **harmlessness**, and **packaging** for extremely short structural text.

# Core assessment thesis

This text is weak as a rich standalone entry, but not worthless. Its strongest value is structural, not explanatory. The owner-faithful reading is therefore:
- low standalone richness
- real retained-context value
- possible duplication value
- final `A` only under that stricter interpretation

# Standalone value vs retained-context value vs duplication value

- **Standalone value:** low. It does not explain the three مباحث, and even the first مبحث is only named, not developed.
- **Retained-context value:** high. When kept with what comes before or after, it helps preserve the author's organizational flow.
- **Duplication value:** real. Keeping `في الحديث ثلاثة مباحث:` in both adjacent excerpts may be justified because it is short, non-obstructive, and structurally informative.

# Added-benefit doctrine

The owner's explicit test is not "does it satisfy a rule on paper?" but "what benefit does it add?" An excerpt that adds no benefit and wastes focus should not survive as a naked atom. Here, the two-line segment adds weak explanatory benefit but can add structural benefit.

# Harmlessness doctrine

Shortness alone is insufficient. The stronger criterion is:
- short or otherwise non-obstructive
- harmless to retain
- capable of adding structural potential

Harmlessness and usefulness are not identical. A piece can be harmless yet still be weak. The owner's final `A` depends on harmless structural value, not on claiming that the line is independently rich.

# Why shortness alone is not the criterion

The raw comments explicitly reject preference-based excerpting. The owner may love long, deep, extensive excerpts, but that does not authorize bending excerpting around taste. A short piece can still deserve preservation; a long piece can still be badly excerpted.

# Why objective excerpting and subjective usefulness are in tension here

This case reveals a real tension:
- subjective reaction: this is weak and nearly useless by itself
- objective constraint: length preference must not control excerpting

The most faithful resolution is not to erase either side, but to say that the line has **low standalone richness** and **legitimate structural value**.

# Candidate packaging patterns

1. **Standalone micro-entry**
   - Defensible only under the owner's objective/harmlessness reading.
2. **Merge with previous excerpt**
   - Preserves transition from the hadith summary into the analytical section.
3. **Merge with following excerpt**
   - Gives `المبحث الأول` immediate content and removes the feeling of emptiness.
4. **Duplicate across adjacent excerpts**
   - Strong current candidate because it preserves structural context while avoiding loss.
5. **Do not preserve at all**
   - Rejected under the owner's final answer because it discards harmless structural context.

# What the surrounding context changes and what it does not change

The full context shows that the line is not random noise: it introduces the move into خلاف العلماء. That increases its retained-context and duplication value.

What the full context does **not** change is the fact that the excerpt, taken as a standalone unit, remains thin. Context explains its role; it does not magically make it a rich autonomous entry.

# What would be reckless to flatten

- Flattening the answer into "short excerpts are good" would be false.
- Flattening it into "this is useless, so reject it" would also be false.
- Treating retained-context value as identical to standalone value would be false.

# What would be reckless to automate blindly

- Auto-keeping every short heading fragment just because it is short.
- Auto-dropping every short heading fragment because it looks weak in isolation.
- Mistaking harmlessness for sufficient benefit.
- Letting user taste for long or short excerpts drive packaging decisions.
