# Owner-faithful judgment

My actual study behavior for this text is not to treat it as the hadith I directly memorize. I treat it as a scholar-book proof witness. I read it carefully, I analyze it, I compare it, and I use it to understand how this scholar presented the proof. But I do not hand over direct memorization authority to this layer.

What I would memorize directly is the authoritative fetched proof layer that should exist beside it. What I would not memorize directly from here is this science-book rendering as though it were the final proof wording.

The help I actually want is not rewriting of the author’s text. I want:
- authoritative fetched proof linkage
- wording-variant comparison
- significance indicators for differences
- study-friendly chunking mainly in the definitive fetched proof layer

The weaker reading of the question would flatten the real issue into “do I like the author’s original wording or do I want reading help?” That is not the real issue. The real issue is layer integrity: what this text is, what it is not, and what role it should play in study and memorization.

# Broader engineering / protocol judgment

The most important proof-layer insight is that scholar-book proof witnesses and authoritative fetched proofs must coexist without collapsing into each other. One preserves how the scholar transmitted and used the proof. The other carries direct memorization confidence.

The most important original-text-preservation insight is that preserving source wording does not mean rejecting help. It means refusing help that mutates the source layer. Help must sit beside the source, not overwrite it.

The most important variant-analysis insight is that wording differences are not all equal. The system must indicate when they are non-significant, when they are significant, and when it is not certain. Otherwise the owner loses the very analytical advantage the library should provide.

The most important methodology-risk insight is that future scholar methodologies and proof/explanation patterns will not all fit a fixed closed rule set. If the system does not escalate uncertainty when it meets unseen patterns, it will become overconfident in exactly the class of cases the owner fears most.

What would be reckless to ignore:
- the distinction between witness layer and authoritative layer
- the trust implications of memorizing from the wrong layer
- the possibility that wording differences change explanation or ruling
- the need to preserve exact source wording in the witness layer
- the need for uncertainty gates in unseen cases

What would be reckless to automate blindly:
- direct memorization from scholar-book proof renderings
- silent source-text restructuring for readability
- automatic variant-significance judgments without uncertainty states
- collapsing witness and authoritative layers into one proof record
- assuming all future methodologies are already covered by today’s rules
