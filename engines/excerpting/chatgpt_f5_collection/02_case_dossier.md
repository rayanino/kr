- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] In Islamic sciences that is especially risky because wording, route, and grading can materially affect the explanation.

# Generated note vs preserved source context

- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Generated note:
  - faster patch
  - lower integrity
  - easier to make lossy
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Source-preserving context:
  - closer to original wording
  - better for integrity
  - stronger anchor for explanation
- [source_basis: model_expansion | owner_relation: model_only] Generated notes should be treated as assistive summaries, not as replacements for context that the source itself can provide.

# Explanation/explained unity doctrine pressure

- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] The owner still likes the *idea* of separating explained text from explanation.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] But this case is strong evidence that the separation is often unsafe in practice.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Main practical risks:
  - hadith wording variants
  - hadith route variants
  - grading sensitivity
  - hunting for which version a scholar had in view
  - scholar-specific framing loss

- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Book-preserved proof excerpts must remain in the library.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] They are not enough by themselves as the final authoritative proof layer.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Authoritative fetched proof records are also needed.
- [source_basis: inferred_from_prior_chat | owner_relation: owner_consistent_inference] The two layers need explicit linkage, not silent substitution.

# Variant / wording / grading sensitivity

- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] A scholar may explain one wording of a hadith, not another.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] A scholar may explain the hadith while presupposing a particular grading or authenticity stance.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Therefore broad note-level identification of “this is about hadith X” may still be too weak.

# Methodology variability and unseen-edge-case doctrine

- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Scholars may interweave rule, proof, and explanation differently.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] Not all future edge cases can be prelisted.
- [source_basis: explicit_draft | owner_relation: owner_explicit] The system therefore needs uncertainty gates and intelligent diagnosis, not only fixed rules.
- [source_basis: model_expansion | owner_relation: model_only] The note question is a good example of why surface readability should never be accepted as proof that the structural decision was safe.
