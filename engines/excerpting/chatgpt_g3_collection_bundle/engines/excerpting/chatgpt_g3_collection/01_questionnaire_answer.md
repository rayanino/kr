My strongest answer is C: it depends on the content. I do not want the library to ask whether a passage is point 2 or point 5 and then excerpt based on that. That is a dangerous way to think. Numbering is just one way an author organizes text. The real question is what the passage is actually doing.

For this specific point, I would say it likely deserves its own excerpt, and I would keep its attached explanation with it. The reason is not that it is one numbered bullet from a larger list. The reason is that it addresses a distinct issue: that the Prophet’s command to Ibn عمر to take her back is evidence that the divorce counted, and then the following lines explain the reasoning and mention the disagreement over whether that command implies obligation or recommendation. That is a real knowledge unit.

So if you ask me in general whether each numbered point should be its own excerpt, my answer is no, not as a rule. But if you ask whether numbered points often end up being their own excerpts, I would say yes, often, because many of them really are separate rulings or separate functions. And if the list had eight points and each point was genuinely about a different ruling, then that would make separation even more likely. Still, the reason would be the different rulings, not the numbering.

At the same time, numbered-list context does create extra reassurance needs. When I study point 2, I naturally wonder how it relates to point 1, point 3, and whatever introduction came before the list. So numbering does matter diagnostically, but not decisively. It raises context questions. It should not control excerpting.
