# Hard Judgment

## Owner-faithful judgment

### Final questionnaire choice
`C`

### Why
Because the real issue is not the mere existence or absence of structure. The real issue is whether guidance contaminates the wrong stage.

### Most dangerous overall failure
Wrong excerpting / knowledge corruption, especially when the taxonomy tree biases excerpting.

### Most dangerous failure inside the narrower A/B/C comparison
Once excerpting is protected, overgranulated placement is more dangerous than undergranulated placement.

### Why excerpt corruption and visible misplacement must not be treated as equivalent
- excerpt corruption can be silent
- silent corruption can survive for years
- one corrupted excerpt can call the whole book and engine into question
- visible misplacement is still severe, but more detectable and usually more recoverable

### Why overgranulation may be more dangerous than undergranulation
Because undergranulation often leaves the missing split visible, while overgranulation forces a harder reassembly puzzle and deeper common-denominator confusion.

### What would be reckless to ignore
- taxonomy-driven excerpting bias
- excerpt output changing when tree granulation changes
- any stage contamination that lets placement convenience rewrite excerpt truth

### What would be reckless to conflate
- excerpt truth vs placement quality
- visible wrong boxes vs silent corruption
- undergranulation blur vs overgranulation fragmentation

## Broader engineering / red-team judgment

The wrong answer to F8 would be a flattened answer.

### Why `A` alone would be too flat
It would correctly notice that premature structure can be dangerous, but it would miss the deeper stage-boundary insight and would not explain why some uses of guidance are beneficial.

### Why `B` alone would be too flat
It would correctly notice that blind processing can become inconsistent or chaotic, but it would understate the asymmetry between visible structural weakness and silent corruption.

### Why `C` is right but still incomplete unless expanded
`C` is correct only if it is interpreted hierarchically:
1. overall danger sits above the A/B/C frame at the excerpting stage
2. then the narrower placement comparison is made
3. then overgranulation wins as the more dangerous placement failure

### Most dangerous areas to defend
- excerpting independence from taxonomy state
- stage boundaries between excerpting, placement, validation, and presentation
- invariance across tree redesigns and granulation changes

### Most dangerous failures to under-prioritize
- placement-fit validation that silently edits excerpt truth
- tree redesign that rewrites already-frozen excerpts
- blind boundary inconsistency that looks tolerable locally but erodes trust globally

### Bottom line
The shallow reading of F8 is “pick A, B, or C.”
The correct reading is:
- overall: excerpt corruption is the real nightmare
- mechanism: stage contamination by the wrong kind of guidance
- within the narrower placement comparison: overgranulation is worse than undergranulation
- therefore: `C`, but only with the hierarchy preserved
