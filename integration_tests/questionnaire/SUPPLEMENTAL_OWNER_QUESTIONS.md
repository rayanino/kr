## SUP-CF-1 — Comparative Madhhab as One Teaching Unit

Read this excerpt:

> اختلاف العلماء:
> اختلف العلماء هل للبائن نفقة وسكنى، زمن العدة، أو لا؟
> فذهب الإمام أحمد: إلى أنه ليس لها نفقة، ولا سكنى...
> وذهب الحنفية إلى أن لها النفقة والسكنى...
> وذهب مالك، والشافعي، إلى أن لها السكنى دون النفقة...
> والصحيح، هو القول الأول، لقوة الدليل وعدم المعارض...

Question:

When a text lays out multiple madhhab positions, their evidences, and a final preference, is the whole comparison itself one unit of knowledge?

Or would you rather the library separate each school’s position into its own entry and link them together?

What would make comparative fiqh most useful to you when you study?

## SUP-AQ-1 — When the Definition Itself Is the Debate

No excerpt to read.

Sometimes a scholarly dispute is not just about a ruling, but about the meaning
of a term itself. For example: what a word means linguistically, what it means
technically, and whether those meanings should be kept together because the
debate depends on both.

Question:

In that kind of case, do you want the library to keep the meanings together
because the relationship between them is the real teaching unit?

Or do you still prefer separate entries with links?

What would make this kind of material safest and most useful for you?
