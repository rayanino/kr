# Placement of المجاز العقلي in a Student-Facing Balāgha Taxonomy

## Why this is a real classification problem

**المجاز العقلي** (also called **المجاز الإسنادي** or **المجاز الحكمي**) is not “metaphor” in the lexical sense (استعمال لفظ في غير ما وضع له), but a **figurative attribution (إسناد)**: an action (or what has the meaning of an action) is predicated of something other than what the speaker takes to be its “true” locus/agent under ظاهر الحال, with a قرينة preventing literal attribution. citeturn20view1

This means any taxonomy has to decide whether it is “about”:

- **the word/lexeme level** (بيان-style: الحقيقة/المجاز and their mechanisms as ways of expressing meaning), or  
- **the sentence predication level** (معاني-style: أحوال الإسناد/المسند/المسند إليه, i.e., how attribution is engineered to fit مقتضى الحال).

The placement conflict you’re facing is therefore not a mere cataloging quirk; it reflects **two different definitions of what “belongs” to علم البيان vs علم المعاني**, and it has downstream consequences for how students learn “majāz” as a concept. citeturn2view1

## What the two canonical frameworks actually do and why

### السكاكي’s placement and its internal logic

In **مفتاح العلوم**, the chapter structure places discussion of **المجاز العقلي** inside the rhetoric portion under **علم البيان**, specifically within **الأصل الثاني من علم البيان في المجاز** (with “في المجاز العقلي” as a subchapter). citeturn19view0

That structural choice fits a “بيان-first” conception: البيان is where the theory of **حقيقة/مجاز** as expressive routes is developed, and المجاز العقلي—despite operating over الإسناد—is still treated as a member of the broader “majāz family.” citeturn19view0

### القزويني’s move and the explicit rationale (plus the famous objection)

In **الإيضاح في علوم البلاغة**, the tradition is explicitly summarized:

- “ذكر الخطيب المجاز العقلي في علم المعاني وذكره السكاكي في علم البيان.” citeturn2view1

Then the book reports the **argument for the move**: because المجاز العقلي is “من أحوال الإسناد,” it can be treated as one of the conditions by which **الكلام المفيد** is tuned to **مقتضى الحال**, and الإسناد is precisely what makes الكلام مفيدًا in the first place. citeturn2view1

Critically, the same passage also records **سعد الدين التفتازاني’s** pushback: even if المجاز العقلي concerns الإسناد, **علم المعاني** (as defined) studies those states “من حيث” they realize مطابقة مقتضى الحال; whereas بحث الحقيقة والمجاز العقليين is not obviously from that “haythiyya,” and if it were, then the lexical reality/metaphor would also intrude into معاني. citeturn2view1

So, even inside the القزويني-centered tradition, you have:

- a **structural decision** (place it under أحوال الإسناد), and  
- an **ongoing theoretical dispute** about whether that placement matches the strict definition of “معاني.” citeturn2view1

This matters for your taxonomy: you are not choosing between “right vs wrong,” but between two “valid” organizational priorities.

## Which placement is the majority practice in classical and modern textbooks

### Classical “textbook tradition” (the post-classical madrasa canon)

If “majority” is measured by the **dominant pedagogical canon** (what was repeatedly copied, taught, commented on, and made into the backbone curriculum), then the gravitational center is **تلخيص المفتاح** and its ecosystem. A modern scholarly working paper on Ottoman madrasa rhetoric teaching states bluntly that **Talkhīṣ al-Miftāḥ was “by far the most popular schoolbook on rhetoric in the madrasa curriculum from the later Middle Ages up to modern times,”** and even adduces library-copy counts as an index of curricular saturation. citeturn18view0

Within that Talkhīṣ-centered pedagogical lineage, the placement is demonstrably **معاني → أحوال الإسناد**. For example, **بغية الإيضاح لتلخيص المفتاح** (a major modern teaching commentary in this lineage) has, in its table of contents, **الفن الأول: علم المعاني → الباب الأول: القول في أحوال الإسناد الخبري → فصل: الحقيقة والمجاز العقليان**. citeturn2view2

So, for “classical textbooks” understood as the enduring *madrasa textbook sequence*, the majority practice is best described as: **Qazwīnī placement dominates the school tradition**, even when individual commentators debate the strict theoretical fit. citeturn18view0turn2view2turn2view1

### Modern printed “student manuals” (widely read simplified البلاغة books)

Modern student-facing manuals split more visibly.

A widely circulated “school classic” in many settings, **جواهر البلاغة**, places **المجاز العقلي** under **علم البيان → الباب الثاني في المجاز → (المبحث الثالث في تعريف المجاز العقلي وعلاقاته)**. citeturn21view0

At the same time, modern curriculum-style works explicitly modeled on the Talkhīṣ order place it under **علم المعاني**. One example is **المنهاج الواضح للبلاغة**, whose contents show: **علم المعاني → أحوال الإسناد الخبري → (… الحقيقة العقلية والمجاز العقلي)**, and then later a separate **علم البيان → مبحث الحقيقة والمجاز** that does not absorb المجاز العقلي into the بيان branch. citeturn22view0

**Bottom line for your Question 1:**  
- **Classical pedagogical canon (Talkhīṣ-centered):** overwhelmingly aligns with **(B) المعاني → أحوال الإسناد**, because Talkhīṣ and its commentarial tradition became the dominant “textbook infrastructure.” citeturn18view0turn2view2turn2view1  
- **Modern simplified textbooks:** you see a real split; influential manuals like **جواهر البلاغة** still teach it under **(A) البيان → المجاز**, while other “curriculum/sequence” works keep the Talkhīṣ-type placement under معاني. citeturn21view0turn22view0  

So the “majority” answer depends on what you count as the relevant population. If you mean “the backbone tradition that most advanced students ultimately encounter,” it strongly favors **(B)**. citeturn18view0turn2view2

## Taxonomy design for students: which placement minimizes cross-reference pain

Your current tree places **المجاز العقلي** under **علم البيان → المجاز** (node path `3ilm_albayan > almajaaz > almajaaz_3aqli`). fileciteturn0file0

To evaluate “cross-reference complications,” there are two distinct kinds of friction:

### Prerequisite-direction friction

- If you place **المجاز العقلي** under **بيان**, students can first learn **الإسناد / المسند / المسند إليه** in معاني (you already have a full node cluster for that), then later encounter “figurative attribution” as an application. This is a **one-way dependency**: بيان can assume isnād terminology already taught. fileciteturn0file0turn20view1

- If you place it under **معاني**, students must either:
  - learn “majāz concepts” (قرينة, حقيقة/مجاز as a framing) early inside معاني, or  
  - be sent forward to بيان’s introductory “الحقيقة والمجاز والقرائن” material and then return.  
  That creates a **back-and-forth dependency loop** unless you duplicate a minimal “قرينة + الحقيقة/المجاز” micro-intro inside معاني’s isnād chapter. citeturn20view1turn2view1

On pure prerequisite flow alone, **(A)** (بيان placement) often yields fewer navigational detours in a tree that is read “معاني → بيان.” fileciteturn0file0

### Homonymy/duplication friction inside “relations lists”

Your current tree lists **علاقات** for both:
- **المجاز المرسل** (بيان) and  
- **المجاز العقلي** (also currently بيان),  

and several labels overlap (e.g., **السببية / المسببية**, and arguably “ظرفية” resembles **المحلية** in student intuition). fileciteturn0file0turn21view0

When both sit under the same “بيان → المجاز” super-branch, students are more likely to conflate:
- “سببية” as a **lexeme/meaning relation** (مجاز مرسل), with  
- “سببية” as a **predication/attribution relation** (مجاز عقلي). citeturn21view0turn20view1

If you move **المجاز العقلي** to **معاني → الإسناد**, the tree structure itself becomes a disambiguation device: in **معاني**, these are “relations of attribution”; in **بيان**, they remain “relations of استعمال اللفظ.” This usually reduces the “same label, different mechanism” confusion in the student’s mental index—even if it introduces one explicit cross-reference link between sciences. citeturn2view1turn21view0turn20view1

**Answer for your Question 2 (fewer cross-reference complications):**  
- If “complication” primarily means **navigation loops and prerequisite flow**, (A) is simpler in a معاني→بيان reading order. fileciteturn0file0  
- If “complication” primarily means **conceptual indexing and avoiding homonymous relation-lists under the same banner**, (B) is cleaner because it separates “lexical majāz” from “isnād majāz” at the top level. citeturn21view0turn20view1turn2view1  

For a taxonomy (tree) meant as a *lookup and mental model* more than a linear course outline, the second kind of complication is usually the more damaging one long-term.

## If you place it under معاني, do you need a بيان cross-reference leaf

Yes—if you care about student usability, you need at least a **pointer** in the بيان “مجاز” area, because students (and many modern manuals) will look for it there by default. citeturn21view0turn2view1

You actually already have an ideal “hook” for this: your بيان node **“تقسيم المجاز: لغوي/عقلي/مركب (بحسب الاصطلاح)”** (`anwa3_almajaz`) is a natural place to attach a note such as:

- “المجاز العقلي (= الإسناد المجازي) يُدرَس هنا/هناك، وانظر: المعاني → الإسناد …”

That avoids duplicating the full subtree while ensuring students can find the topic from the “majāz entry point.” fileciteturn0file0

Pedagogically, this mirrors how Qazwīnī-era framing often distinguishes between:
- “حقيقة/مجاز” as applied to **الألفاظ** (بيان), and
- “حقيقة/مجاز” as applied to **الإسناد/المعاني** (معاني), while acknowledging terminological overlap. citeturn2view1turn20view1

## Concrete recommendation

### Recommendation

Place **المجاز العقلي** primarily under:

**(B) المعاني → الإسناد → (أحوال الإسناد / الحقيقة والمجاز العقليان)**

and keep **one explicit cross-reference note** under:

**البيان → المجاز → تقسيم المجاز (لغوي/عقلي/مركب)**

### Scholarly justification

This matches the **dominant textbook-canon organization** anchored in **تلخيص المفتاح** as the most widely taught rhetoric schoolbook across centuries, where **الحقيقة والمجاز العقليان** sits in the opening isnād discussions. citeturn18view0turn2view2turn20view1

It also aligns with the core analytic fact that “المجاز العقلي” is **a property of الإسناد** (how predication is assigned) rather than a property of a single word’s استعمال. That is exactly why the “move to معاني” was argued in the first place: it is “من أحوال الإسناد.” citeturn2view1turn20view1

Even acknowledging **التفتازاني’s** objection that this may not strictly fall under معاني’s defining “haythiyya,” the teaching tradition still standardizes the placement because isnād is the natural locus for explaining it and applying it across خبر/إنشاء and related constructions. In other words: the *theoretical* border is debatable, but the *pedagogical* border has already been operationalized for centuries in the canonical curriculum sequence. citeturn2view1turn18view0

### Why this is the best student-facing compromise

- It prevents students from equating “سببية” in مجاز مرسل with “سببية” in إسناد مجازي just because both sit under “بيان→مجاز.” The tree’s top-level partition (معاني vs بيان) becomes a durable “type system.” citeturn21view0turn20view1  
- It keeps discoverability high by adding a single cross-reference at the point where students expect to find it (the “majāz” branch), which is crucial given the ongoing presence of the بيان-placement in influential modern manuals. citeturn21view0turn2view1  
- It aligns your taxonomy with what advanced students will encounter when they consult the Talkhīṣ-centered heritage commentaries and their modern pedagogical descendants. citeturn18view0turn2view2
