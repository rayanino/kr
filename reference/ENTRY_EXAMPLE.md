### تعريف المبتدأ — Definition of the Subject

**Prerequisites:** الاسم والفعل والحرف (parts of speech classification), الإعراب (case inflection basics), مفهوم العامل (concept of grammatical operators)

**Where this fits:** This is the entry point for باب المبتدأ والخبر (the chapter on subject-predicate sentences), which is foundational to understanding الجملة الاسمية (nominal sentences). Everything in Arabic sentence analysis builds on whether you're looking at a nominal or verbal sentence — and this definition determines what counts as the subject of a nominal sentence.

**Why this matters:** The definition of المبتدأ is not just a technical formality — it encodes a fundamental disagreement about HOW Arabic grammar works. The Basran school built grammar on meaning (the subject is what you're talking ABOUT). The Kufan school built it on form (the subject is what occupies a certain position). This difference ripples through dozens of other grammatical rules.

#### The Core Definition

المبتدأ is a noun (اسم) in the nominative case (مرفوع) that forms the first part of a nominal sentence (الجملة الاسمية), about which the predicate (الخبر) provides information.

#### The Scholarly Positions

**The Basran definition — meaning-based:**
سيبويه (d. 180 AH) in الكتاب — the first systematic Arabic grammar, composed during the formative period when grammatical terminology was still being established — defines المبتدأ as: "the noun that the خبر is built upon" (الاسم الذي بُني عليه الخبر). This is a semantic definition: the subject is identified by its ROLE in meaning (what is being talked about).

ابن السراج (d. 316 AH), writing الأصول في النحو 136 years later when the Basran school had fully systematized, refined this to: "the noun that is free from verbal operators" (الاسم المجرد من العوامل اللفظية). This refinement explicitly invokes العامل theory — the Basran framework where grammatical case is "caused" by operators. The subject is the noun that gets its nominative case from the abstract meaning of الابتداء (inception) rather than from any verbal operator. Ibn al-Sarraj was a student of المبرد (d. 286 AH), who was himself a student of الجرمي, who studied with الأخفش الأوسط — Sibawayhi's own student. This four-generation chain preserved and formalized Sibawayhi's original insight.

**The Kufan position — form-based:**
الكسائي (d. 189 AH) and الفراء (d. 207 AH) — the founders of the Kufan school, contemporaries of Sibawayhi working in a rival intellectual tradition — used the term المُسنَد إليه (the thing predicated about) rather than المبتدأ. Their approach was more positional: the subject is identified by WHERE it appears in the sentence and what syntactic relationship it has with the predicate, rather than by abstract operator theory.

**The post-classical consensus:**
By the time of ابن مالك (d. 672 AH), writing the ألفية nearly 500 years after Sibawayhi, the Basran definition had become standard. Ibn Malik defines it in verse 75: "المبتدا اسم ابتدا" — the subject is a noun of inception. His commentator ابن عقيل (d. 769 AH) explains that this incorporates both Sibawayhi's semantic core and Ibn al-Sarraj's operator refinement. Virtually all post-classical grammars follow this merged definition.

#### Edge Cases and Common Misunderstandings

**Edge case 1: المبتدأ المؤخر (delayed subject).** In sentences like "في الدار رجلٌ" (In the house is a man), رجلٌ is the مبتدأ even though it comes AFTER the predicate. This breaks the "beginning of sentence" part of the naive definition. The Basran analysis handles this: the subject is defined by its semantic role (what's being informed about), not its position.

**Edge case 2: ضمائر الشأن (pronouns of the matter).** In "هو زيدٌ قائمٌ" (the matter is: Zayd is standing), هو is a مبتدأ that doesn't refer to anything specific. This is a test case that distinguishes the meaning-based definition from the form-based one.

**Common misunderstanding:** Students often confuse المبتدأ with الفاعل (the doer in verbal sentences). Both are مرفوع (nominative), but المبتدأ begins a nominal sentence while الفاعل follows a verb. The distinction is structural, not semantic — the same noun can be مبتدأ or فاعل depending on sentence structure.

After understanding المبتدأ, proceed to: تعريف الخبر (definition of the predicate) → أنواع الخبر (types of predicates) → نواسخ المبتدأ والخبر (operators that modify the subject-predicate structure: كان وأخواتها، إنّ وأخواتها).
