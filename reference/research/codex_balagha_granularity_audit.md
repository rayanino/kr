# Balagha Granularity Audit

## Proposed Leaf Criterion

A balagha leaf must be rhetoric-first, conceptually self-standing, stable across books, and free of nahw/tafsir/adab/lexicography bleed. A candidate leaf is production-ready only if it appears as an explicit subtopic in the deep-analyzed books and is supported either by corpus frequency (book_count >= 5) or by recurrence in at least 2 of the 3 analyzed books.

## Proposed Anti-Overgranulation Rule

Do not promote exercises, answer keys, biography/history blocks, examples, proof fragments, generic wrappers, or book-local subcases into production leaves. If a division is mainly a presentation shell or survives in only one analyzed book without corpus support, exclude it.

## Nahw-Overlap Risks

- **أحوال الإسناد الخبري** — Risk against nahw باب المبتدأ والخبر and أقسام الخبر; keep only rhetoric-first forms such as أغراض الخبر and أضرب الخبر.
- **الإنشاء** — Risk against nahw operator chapters for الاستفهام والنداء والتمني والأمر والنهي; exclude grammar-first or operator-first subcases.
- **تعريف المسند إليه** — Risk against nahw النكرة والمعرفة وأقسام المعارف; exclude any split child whose concern is definiteness mechanics rather than rhetorical effect.
- **تقديم المسند إليه** — Risk against nahw تقديم الخبر وتأخيره and related constituent order chapters; keep only rhetoric-first المسند/المسند إليه formulations.
- **حذف المسند إليه** — Risk against nahw حذف المبتدأ أو الخبر; exclude any child framed as sentence-grammar ellipsis rather than rhetorical omission.
